The Ancient Land of Bihar

The name 'Bihar' originates from "Vihara," meaning monastery, a testament to its past as a great center of Buddhist and Jain learning. It'''s the land where Lord Buddha attained enlightenment and where the mighty Mauryan and Gupta empires flourished, shaping the course of Indian history. From the ancient university of Nalanda to its pivotal role in the freedom struggle, Bihar'''s legacy is a rich tapestry of knowledge, power, and revolution.

Vibrant Festivals

The rhythm of life in Bihar is marked by a calendar of colorful and deeply spiritual festivals.

Chhath Puja

The most significant festival, an ancient Vedic tradition where devotees worship the Sun God, Surya, offering prayers for life, prosperity, and well-being.

Sama Chakeva

Celebrated in Mithila, this festival marks the beautiful bond between brothers and sisters, as siblings craft clay birds and perform rituals.

Makar Sankranti

Also known as 'Tila Sankranti,' this harvest festival is celebrated with feasts of 'Dahi-Chura' (curd and beaten rice) and sweets made of sesame (til).

Delectable Cuisine

The heart and soul of Bihari culture, expressed through unforgettable flavors.

Litti Chokha

The iconic dish of Bihar. Wheat dough balls are stuffed with a spicy mixture of roasted gram flour (sattu), then roasted over fire and served with mashed vegetables (chokha).

Dal Pitha

Steamed rice flour dumplings stuffed with a savory lentil filling, a healthy and beloved snack.

Thekua

A crisp, sweet biscuit made from wheat flour and jaggery, traditionally prepared as a sacred offering during Chhath Puja.

Traditional Attire

A blend of comfort, tradition, and elegance woven into the fabric of daily life.

For Women

The Sari is the quintessential attire, with the 'Seedha Aanchal' draping style being a distinctive feature of Bihar. The state is also the heartland of Tussar Silk, producing exquisite sarees known for their rich texture and natural golden sheen.

For Men

The traditional attire for men is the comfortable and elegant Dhoti, paired with a Kurta. This classic ensemble is a common sight in rural areas and during festivals and formal occasions.
