Pearls of Wisdom (Padhua)

A comprehensive collection of timeless proverbs from Bihar, offering insights into its culture, values, and humor.

"अधजल गगरी छलकत जाय"

An empty vessel makes much noise; people with little knowledge often boast the most.

"जेकर लाठी, ओकर भঁইস"

He who holds the stick, owns the buffalo; a commentary on "might is right".

"घर के मुरगी दाल बराबर"

Self-possessions are always undermined; familiarity breeds contempt.

"नाच ना जाने, आँगन टेढ़"

Doesn't know how to dance, but blames the crooked courtyard; a bad workman blames his tools.

"सौ सोनार के, एक लोहार के"

A single blow from a blacksmith is as effective as a hundred from a goldsmith; quality over quantity.

"बन्दर क्या जाने अदरक का स्वाद"

What does a monkey know of the taste of ginger; casting pearls before swine.

"केतनो सोझ करू, कुकुर के पुछ टेढ़े रहत"

You can't straighten a dog's tail; some people will never change.

"आगे नाथ ना पीछे पगहा"

With no one to control them; someone who has no responsibilities.

"चमड़ी जाये पर दमड़ी ना जाये"

The skin may go, but the penny shouldn't; about a very miserly person.

"जबरा मारे रोवे ना दे"

The powerful one hits you and doesn't even let you cry; oppression by the powerful.

"अंधा बांटे रेवड़ी, फिर फिर अपनो को दे"

The blind distributes sweets, and gives them only to his own; about favoritism and nepotism.

"आम के आम गुठली के दाम"

Getting the price of both the mango and its seed; a double benefit or a win-win situation.

"एक अनार सौ बीमार"

One pomegranate for a hundred sick people; a single solution for too many problems, or high demand for a scarce resource.

"कहाँ राजा भोज, कहाँ गंगू तेली"

Where is King Bhoj, and where is Gangu the oil-presser; a comparison between two people of vastly different statuses.

"नौ नगद न तेरह उधार"

Nine in cash is better than thirteen on credit; a bird in the hand is worth two in the bush.

"अईसन कोई ना भेड़ा, जे अपने टांग تरे ना हेरा"

No sheep looks for grass under its own feet; one often overlooks the value of what is close to them.

"आपन सुधि ना, दोसरा के ज्ञान"

Lacks self-awareness but gives knowledge to others; a person who doesn't practice what they preach.

"बिना पेंदी के लोटा"

A pot without a base; a person who is unreliable or constantly changes their opinion.

"पेट में दाढ़ी होना"

To have a beard in the stomach; being wise or mature beyond one's age.

"पानी में रह कर मगर से बैर"

Living in the water and having enmity with the crocodile; it is foolish to make enemies with those in power around you.
